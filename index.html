A.P.J Abdul kalam

1931 - 2015

Missile Man of India

APJ Abdul Kalam, widely known as the "Missile Man of India," was a renowned scientist and former President of India. He earned this title for his significant contributions to the development of ballistic missile and launch vehicle technology in India. Kalam played a crucial role in the development of India's first indigenous satellite launch vehicle and ballistic missiles, including the Agni and Prithvi missiles. He was also instrumental in India's nuclear weapons tests in 1998. Kalam's dedication to advancing India's missile technology earned him widespread admiration and respect. He was awarded the Bharat Ratna, India's highest civilian honor, in 1997 for his outstanding achievements in science and engineering. Kalam's visionary leadership and relentless pursuit of excellence have left a lasting impact on India's defense capabilities and technological advancement. He continues to inspire millions of people, especially the youth, to pursue careers in science and technology.

Biographies

  • APJ Abdul Kalam was born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, India.
  • He studied physics and aerospace engineering, earning degrees from institutions such as St. Joseph's College and the Madras Institute of Technology.
  • Kalam joined the Defense Research and Development Organisation (DRDO) in 1958, where he played a key role in India's missile development program.
  • He served as the Project Director for India's first indigenous satellite launch vehicle, SLV-III, which successfully deployed the Rohini satellite in orbit in 1980.
  • Kalam later became the Chief Executive of the Integrated Guided Missile Development Programme (IGMDP), overseeing the development of missiles like Agni and Prithvi.
